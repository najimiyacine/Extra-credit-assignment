Foreign Investment, Value Addition & National Legislation

Country Foreign Investment Domestic Value‑Added Key Regulation / Export Controls Source
Morocco State‑led OCP Group; Fortescue (Australia) JV for green hydrogen/ammonia. High – phosphate model being replicated for other minerals; green manufacturing hub planned. Export taxes on raw ores to incentivise local processing; licensing under state monopoly. DeHeng Law: Africa Mining Investment Guide (2024)
Nigeria Chinese, Australian, Canadian juniors exploring lithium, REEs. Very low – near‑total export of raw ores; new policy aims to force change. Policy shift (2024): Plan to ban raw mineral exports to require local processing, inspired by Indonesia's nickel success. Mining legal framework covered in national investment guide. DeHeng Law: Nigeria mining legal framework
Gabon French (Eramet), Chinese (CICMHZ), Indian firms in manganese. Moderate – sintering before export; no battery‑grade refining yet. 2019 Mining Code: State holds 10% free carry interest, rights to acquire additional 25%. Mining code amendment trend documented in 2024 study. China Academy of Natural Resources Economics: African Mining Law Evolution (2024)
Zimbabwe Overwhelmingly Chinese (Sinomine, Huayou, Chengxin, Yahua, Tsingshan). Increasing – government‑forced construction of lithium sulphate plants; Huayou built a $400M plant. • Ban on raw lithium ore exports (Dec 2022).
• Exports suspended Feb 2026 for malpractices.
• New export quotas & 10% export tax on concentrates.
• Full concentrate export ban from 1 Jan 2027 unless lithium sulphate plants built. 		CNBC Africa: 2026 export quotas & 2027 ban
DeHeng Law: Zimbabwe legal framework
South Africa Anglo American, Glencore, Sibanye‑Stillwater; growing Chinese interest. Moderate‑High – PGM/manganese refineries; vanadium electrolyte plant for VRFBs. Broad‑Based Black Economic Empowerment (BEE) licensing; Mine Health and Safety Amendment Bill to strengthen enforcement (2024). DeHeng Law: South Africa mining legal framework
CANRE: African Mining Law Evolution Study
DRC Over 70% Chinese (CMOC, CNMC); also Glencore (Swiss), ERG (Kazakh). Very low – world's top cobalt miner but processes almost none domestically. 2018 Mining Code: State free carry increased from 5% to 10%, additional 5% each renewal. Export ban on copper/cobalt concentrates (2024) to force domestic refining; 10% export tax on unprocessed minerals. CANRE: DRC Mining Code provisions
Zambia Glencore, First Quantum (Canada), UAE, increasing Chinese investment. Moderate‑High – Africa's largest copper refiner; exports mainly cathode. 2024 Minerals Regulation Commission Bill: New regulatory body established. Copper royalty rates: 4% to 10% based on price. 15% export tax on concentrates; citizen empowerment requirements for licences under 1000 hectares. Guantao Law Firm: Zambia Mining Investment Legal Guide (2025)
Chambers & Partners: Zambia MRC Bill Review (2024)
Rwanda EU and Chinese investment in tantalum/tungsten traceability & processing. Increasing – aims to be a regional processing hub (new nickel‑cobalt refinery). Mandatory iTSCi blockchain‑based traceability for all 3T mineral exports; the most rigorous licensing regime in the region. iTSCi Programme official site
Tanzania Chinese, Australian (Walkabout, Peak), UK (Pensana for REEs). Very low – graphite/REE projects still at mine stage. 2017 Mining Acts: Mandatory 16% government free‑carried interest in all mining projects; Parliament approval required for investor agreements; government can acquire up to 50% total stake; export ban on unprocessed minerals. CANRE: Tanzania Mining Law provisions
Mozambique Syrah Resources (Australia), Chinese investment in graphite. Moderate – Syrah's US‑based Vidalia processing plant; limited domestic processing. Stable licensing regime; government encourages local beneficiation but no export bans enacted. Production disrupted by civil unrest (2024). DeHeng Law: Mozambique investment framework
Madagascar Chinese (graphite), Sumitomo/Korea (Ambatovy nickel‑cobalt), Australian (REEs). Low – Ambatovy refinery faces repeated disruptions; graphite exported as flake. Mining code revision process ongoing; 2022 draft addressed fiscal regime, gold trafficking and industrial transformation requirements. DeHeng Law: Madagascar mining legal guide
